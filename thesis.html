How do you land a nano-quadcopter on the ceiling?

The answer to that question is highly debateable, especially amongst the members in our lab. However, the way we thought of trying to solve that issue is by taking inspiration from biology, specifically an insect most would consider a pest, a fly.


In terms of engineered flight we still have plenty to learn from nature, and this project is only one aspect where human engineered flight and robotics can improve with a little help from evolution. Because flies are considered pests not many would take time to think about how even though their brain is the size of the head of a needle they are still able to complete impressive aerial manuevers. Because of this the main goal of this project is to take inspiration from blue bottle flies and implement an inverted landing strategy on a nano-quadcopter. In order to complete this task the first question is well where do you even start adapting an acrobatic flier onto an under actuated quadcopter?
